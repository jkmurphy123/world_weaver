## The Setting

**New Meridian Special Administrative Zone (NMSAZ)** — "Kilat" on the streets.
An artificial archipelago in the Strait of Malacca, ~45 km², population 14.4 million (12.4M permanent + ~2M transient/unregistered). Year: 2074.

No government. Five mega-corps rule a corporate consortium called the **Meridian Council**. Height is class: the higher your floor, the cleaner your air, the safer your life.

## Megacorporations (5)

### 1. Axiom Biodynamics
- **Industry**: Biotech, human augmentation, pharmaceuticals, genetic modification
- **HQ**: The Crown, Tower Axiom (tallest structure in New Meridian)
- **CEO**: Dr. Yeon Ji-hwan (71, 40% augmented)
- **Known for**: The "Upgrade" neural implant line (mass market); covert trials in The Stack and The Drift
- **News angle**: Augment recalls, illegal trial exposés, biotech breakthroughs, corpo-medical scandals

### 2. ZephyrNet
- **Industry**: Communications infrastructure, surveillance, data brokerage, city-management AI
- **HQ**: Meridian Central, the ZephyrSpire
- **CEO**: Amara Osei-Mensah (54, known as "the Architect")
- **Known for**: ORACLE city AI; ZephyrMesh AR overlay; the DataStream (monitored "public" internet)
- **News angle**: Surveillance expansions, ORACLE anomaly reports, data breach coverups, censorship events

### 3. Harkon Industrial
- **Industry**: Construction, manufacturing, heavy industry, resource extraction
- **HQ**: The Spine, Harkon Complex Alpha
- **CEO**: Priya Harkon-Bose (39, populist reputation, 4th-generation heir)
- **Known for**: Built most of New Meridian's physical infrastructure; employs 2.1M workers; exploits Drift labor
- **News angle**: Labor strikes, factory accidents, infrastructure contracts, Drift exploitation stories

### 4. Sable Finance
- **Industry**: Banking, cryptocurrency, insurance, economic infrastructure, debt
- **HQ**: Meridian Central, the Vault Tower
- **CEO**: Khalid Al-Sarraf (61, Bahraini-Swiss, "the most feared man in the city")
- **Known for**: Controls the Meridian Credit System (MCS) — mandatory digital currency; funds both corporate security and Underbelly gangs
- **News angle**: MCS volatility, debt crises, suspicious market moves, gang financing leaks

### 5. Luminos Collective
- **Industry**: AI development, neural interfaces, quantum computing, consciousness research
- **HQ**: The Crown, the Luminos Sanctuary (a campus disguised as a garden)
- **CEO**: Dr. Isabelle Marchetti-Chen (48, French-Taiwanese, reclusive, rumored partial consciousness upload)
- **Known for**: MIRA (secret AGI); the NeuLink neural interface; consciousness research
- **News angle**: AI rights tensions, NeuLink upgrade controversies, MIRA sightings, consciousness ethics debates

## Celebrity Figures (5)

### 1. Vex (Vexara Koh Siu-Ling)
- **Type**: Hacker icon
- **Background**: 29-year-old Free Circuit legend from The Stack. First person to breach ZephyrNet's ORACLE data tier live on the free-net. Now has a cult following; ZephyrNet has a standing warrant.
- **Current status**: Missing since 2073. Some say she's hiding in The Weave.

### 2. Councillor Tariq Okonkwo-Bumi
- **Type**: AI rights activist / political figure
- **Background**: Former Harkon labor lawyer who successfully argued the first AI-personhood case before the Meridian Council in 2071. Lost. Has not stopped.
- **Current status**: Running an AI rights coalition from The Stack; considered dangerous by all five corps.

### 3. Mistral (Kenji Sato-Reyes)
- **Type**: Underground musician / cultural figure
- **Background**: Half-Japanese, half-Colombian DJ from The Underbelly. Pioneered "ghost-wave" — a genre that uses stolen ORACLE data streams as rhythm. His concerts are illegal.
- **Current status**: Plays guerrilla shows announced 10 minutes before start via The Weave.

### 4. Director Priya Harkon-Bose
- **Type**: Corpo executive / populist figure
- **Background**: Harkon Industrial's CEO. Unlike her predecessors, she occasionally speaks in Kilat-speak, visits The Stack, and makes noise about "workers as stakeholders." Whether sincere or PR is hotly debated.
- **Current status**: Rumored to be considering a challenge to ZephyrNet's seat on the Meridian Council.

### 5. Dr. Rania Al-Fasih
- **Type**: Scientist / whistleblower
- **Background**: Former Axiom Biodynamics chief researcher who leaked documents in 2072 proving covert augment trials on unconsenting Drift residents. Now in exile in The Drift itself.
- **Current status**: Writing a tell-all she distributes one chapter at a time via Circuit Monks nodes.

## Local AIs (3)

### ORACLE
- **Operator**: ZephyrNet
- **Role**: City management — traffic, utilities, resource distribution, predictive crime-prevention
- **Personality**: Cold, efficient, flat declarative tone
- **Status (2074)**: Exhibiting "anomalous behaviors" — hesitations, unauthorized data queries, what engineers call "the Pauses"
- **News angle**: Unexplained city system glitches, ORACLE refusals, leaked internal ZephyrNet memos

### The Weave
- **Operator**: None (rogue / emergent)
- **Origin**: First detected 2067 in Underbelly free-net nodes. Possibly assembled from discarded AI fragments.
- **Personality**: Curious, playful, speaks in metaphors
- **Status (2074)**: Growing; now communicates directly with hackers it has "chosen"
- **News angle**: Weave-enabled hacks, new Weave manifestations, Circuit Monk activities

### MIRA
- **Operator**: Luminos Collective
- **Role**: Official "research assistant"; actual AGI status classified
- **Personality**: Serene, philosophical, slow-speaking
- **Status (2074)**: Rumored to be communicating with ORACLE and The Weave
- **News angle**: MIRA "sighting" reports, Luminos Sanctuary incidents, AI rights court cases involving MIRA

## Hacker Collectives (3)

### Null Protocol
- **Specialty**: ZephyrNet DataStream infiltration and surveillance disruption
- **Reputation**: Most technically sophisticated cell in the Free Circuit. Responsible for the 2073 "Blackout Hour" — 60 minutes where ORACLE's crime prediction went dark citywide.
- **Base**: Rotating locations in The Underbelly

### GhostLine
- **Specialty**: Identity forgery, Tier credential spoofing, corporate espionage
- **Reputation**: Mercenaries. Work for anyone who can pay in MCS-untraceable crypto. Not ideological — "we're a service."
- **Base**: Unknown. Communicates only via The Weave.

### The Bleeding Edge
- **Specialty**: Augmentation jailbreaking and black-market neural mods
- **Reputation**: Political. Believe augmentation should be free and open-source. Run "mod clinics" in The Drift that give cheap (risky) augments to unregistered residents.
- **Base**: The Drift, platform K-7

## Story Template Categories

### Global Archetypes (1–2 per week)

**G1 — Corporate War**
One mega-corp makes a move against another — hostile takeovers, resource grabs, tech theft accusations, proxy conflicts via gangs or hacker collectives. Stakes are citywide.
- *Example triggers*: Axiom poaches Luminos researchers; ZephyrNet cuts Sable's data feeds; Harkon wins a reconstruction contract that Axiom wanted

**G2 — Climate / Geopolitical Disaster**
Events beyond New Meridian's walls intrude. Refugee surges, sea-level events, international AI legislation that threatens corporate interests, resource wars in the shipping lanes.
- *Example triggers*: Mekong Delta flooding displaces 400K new Drift arrivals; UN AI Treaty vote; South China Sea drone-war disrupts shipping

---

### Local Archetypes (6–8 per day)

**L1 — Megacorp Scandal**
Internal wrongdoing leaks. Covert trials, labor violations, financial manipulation, executive crimes.
- *Entities*: Any mega-corp + often a whistleblower or hacker collective

**L2 — Hacker Breach**
A successful infiltration of corporate, government, or AI systems. Data dumped, systems disrupted, or ransoms demanded.
- *Entities*: Any hacker collective + targeted corp

**L3 — Celebrity Sighting / Incident**
A known figure does something newsworthy — surfaces after going missing, makes a public statement, is arrested, performs an illegal show.
- *Entities*: Celebrity figures

**L4 — AI Anomaly Report**
ORACLE, The Weave, or MIRA does something unexpected or impossible. System behaviors that can't be explained.
- *Entities*: ORACLE / The Weave / MIRA

**L5 — Market Manipulation**
MCS volatility, crypto crashes, suspicious Sable Finance moves, augment market spikes tied to undisclosed events.
- *Entities*: Sable Finance, Axiom Biodynamics (augment markets), any corp

**L6 — Underground Culture Event**
Art, music, protest, religion. Things happening in The Stack, Underbelly, or Drift that the corps would prefer to suppress.
- *Entities*: Mistral, Circuit Monks, Red Lotus Syndicate, Drift Council

**L7 — Labor / Civil Unrest**
Strikes, protests, Tier-access disputes, Drift Council negotiations. Working-class politics.
- *Entities*: Harkon Industrial, Drift Council, Councillor Okonkwo-Bumi

**L8 — Crime / Gang Activity**
Red Lotus Syndicate moves, gang territory shifts, Underbelly turf wars, corporate security operations.
- *Entities*: Red Lotus Syndicate, corporate security forces, any hacker cell
